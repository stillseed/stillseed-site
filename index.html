Our Philosophy

Presence is not something you find. It is something you cultivate.

The Seed of Awareness

Every practice at Stillseed begins with a simple truth: stillness is not the absence of movement, but the presence of attention. Whether you are sitting in formal meditation, following the breath, or watching water bloom through coffee grounds — the work is the same.

Over more than ten years of study with Buddhist teachers across traditions, I have learned that the deepest transformations happen not in retreat centers alone, but in the quiet rituals of daily life. This is why Stillseed weaves together classical meditation techniques — Shamatha, Vipassana, and conscious breathwork — with embodied practices like mindful coffee brewing.

We do not teach escape. We teach return. Return to the body. Return to the breath. Return to this moment, exactly as it is.

About the Teacher

Orgyen Thupten Dhargye

(Mark Low) · Founder of Stillseed

I came to meditation not seeking peace, but seeking truth. Over more than a decade of practice under various Buddhist teachers, I have explored the depth of Shamatha and Vipassana meditation, breathwork disciplines, and the contemplative arts.

Stillseed was born from a simple observation: the skills we cultivate on the cushion — attention, patience, equanimity — are the same skills we need at work, in relationships, and in the quiet moments before sleep. The question was never how to escape daily life, but how to meet it fully.

My teaching weaves classical Buddhist meditation techniques with embodied, sensory-based practices. The mindful coffee brewing session, for example, grew from years of noticing how the simple ritual of preparing a cup could become a complete meditation — if approached with the right intention.

I teach in English and am based in Klang Valley, Selangor, Malaysia. Sessions are available in-person and online.
